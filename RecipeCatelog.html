Best Appetizers Ever!

The word itself, antipasto, means "before the meal," from the roots anti-, "before," and pasto, "food." The plural of antipasto is antipasti. Definitions of antipasto. a course of appetizers in an Italian meal. type of: appetiser, appetizer, starter.

Here's the Main Dishes...

The primo: In Italy, pasta is a first course, or primo, served as an appetizer, not as the main event. Soup, rice, and polenta are the other options for the primo. The secondo: The main course is called il secondo, or the second course. Chicken, meat, or fish are the usual choices, and portions are generally small.

What a Sweetness!

In Italy, il dolce is not only a sweet way to end a lunch or dinner—it's also the way many Italians start their day. The term dolci literally means “sweets,” though the French term “dessert” is also frequently used in Italy.
